Our Projects

Here are some of the exciting projects created by students across different VR Clubs. Students have spent their time learning to create their own games in Unity, as well as experiencing and exploring VR technology.

Club members work together to improve each others' projects and create something they're proud of, showcasing the innovative potential of VR in education.

Mixed Reality Science

One of the VR Club leaders, Omkar Bharath, spent the summer of 2023 building three VR science experiments. Participants can assemble a lemon battery, perform a flame color test on different chemicals, and watch mold grow on bread.

This was the flagship project that launched the creation of a VR Club in the first place.

AI Lab Assistant

Ram, another VR Club leader, created a virtual lab assistant over 2023 and 2024. The lab assistant answers science questions on almost any topic, and can clarify any doubts.

Ram's project synergizes with all previous and future projects to display a whole new dimension of learning.

ASU Sports Drink Lab

Tahlia, a VR Club Leader at Paradise Valley High School, developed a full lab experience based on Arizona State University (ASU) curricula.

The lab works the same as a physical one, and saves plenty of resources.

AI Scheduler

Diego, a VR Club Leader at HYSA, created an AI scheduler on a raspberry pi.

The goal is to develop a simple AI assistant that users can wear as a pin to schedule meetings.
